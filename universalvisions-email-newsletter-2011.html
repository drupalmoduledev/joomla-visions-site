What are Prayer Beads?
Many religious and otherwise spiritual traditions use prayer beads in most corners of the world. The calm elicited by focusing on a tangible object while meditating on spiritual goals, venturing to clear one's mind of outside distractions, is a simple description. Both, quantities and materials can be important if one adheres strictly to particular traditions. Among the materials we have at the shop include Tibetan red sandalwood with turquois;, tulasi (a light wood); and rudrausha.

An Eastern Tradition Illuminated

"A Japa mala or mala ...is a set of beads commonly used by Hindus and Buddhists, usually made from 108 beads...Buddhist prayer beads are a traditional tool used to count the number of times a mantra is recited whilst meditating. They are similar to other forms of prayer beads used in various world religions; thus some call this tool the Buddhist rosary." Why are there 108 beads? "In traditional Buddhist thought, people are said to have 108 afflictions or klesas....There are six senses (sight, sound, smell, taste, touch, and consciousness) multiplied by three reactions (positive, negative, or indifference) making 18 "feelings." Each of these feelings can be either "attached to pleasure or detached from pleasure" making 36 "passions", each of which may be manifested in the past, present, or future.... All the combinations of all these things makes a total of 108". {ref: wikipedia}
